WELCOME

Hi, my name is Liam

I am a 21 year old developer, musician, and geek from Virginia Beach, VA.

I am currently a fourth-year student of computer science at James Madison University, in Harrisonburg, VA. I have interned with Cloudera for the past two summers (2014-2015), working on the IT Ops team my first summer, and building data science applications in python for the Cloudera Manager team the next.

In my spare time, I compose and produce music under the pseudonym datamancer, and compete in various collegiate hackathons. I started a student organization last year called Madison Hip Hop Collective, or MHHC. My music ranges from instrumental hip hop to film scores, and I have placed highly in or won awards in a few different hackathons.

Prior to my internships with Cloudera, I played baritone for three summers with the 10-time DCI World Champion Cadets Drum and Bugle corps, and have performed in or visited to nearly every state in US, save for North Dakota, Alaska, and Hawaii.

My passion for music and technology has inspired me to actively seek out a role as an audio software developer.

PROJECTS

Syncspin

Syncspin is a music streaming web application that uses a voting system and a recommendation engine for crowdsourced curation of a playlist. Created with parties and large events in mind, Syncspin allows many people to collaborate on a playlist in real time.

Built using the Beats Music API, node.js, bootstrap, and socket.io for the real time voting system.

Syncspin won "Best use of Intel Mashery API" at HackGT 2014.

DCI Scores Bot

DCI Scores Bot is a bot which posts the results of Drum Corps International competition results to reddit

Built using Python, PRAW, and BeautifulSoup.

TakeRoot

TakeRoot is an android app that allows users to post information about their home-grown produce.

Users can browse listings of local produce in their community, and use the app to get in contact with the growers.

Built at BluestoneHacks 2015 using Android and Parse

JMUSE Player

JMUSE is a many-format web browser audio player and audio visualizer.

JMUSE allows for WAV, MP3, MP4, FLAC, and OGG playback using the JS WebAudio API. The WebAudio API is used in conjunction with an HTML5 canvas element to provide hardware accelerated FFT visualization.

Created in conjunction with three other JMU students in March 2015 for CS345, Software Engineering.

PlaytRate

PlaytRate is a web application which mines twitter data for mentions of a Yelp-listed business, performs sentiment analysis on the tweets, and calculates a numeric score in the form of "golden plates"

Built using the Beats Music API, node.js, bootstrap, and socket.io for the real time voting system.

MUSIC

I make a variety of different music using mostly Logic Pro X and a smattering of outboard gear. I take pride in learning all of the functions and subtleties of the tools I use to make music, be it the latest DAW update, or an ancient, clunky, 8-bit sampler. I design all of my own synth patches, I don't use loops/sample packs, and I do not pirate audio software. My experience as an instrumental musician means I prefer to play all of the individual parts that make up my arrangements, using my MPC sequencer to record and cycle MIDI data in real time. I generally detest clicking around in a DAW, and prefer a more tactile creative approach, but I still mix my performances using Logic Pro. The innovations made in music technology in just the past few decades are awe inspiring. The tools in the average producer's arsenal provide a virtually unlimited palette of sounds and textures, pushing sonic creativity to the limits of the human imagination.

Here is a list of my current noisemakers, in no particular order.

  • Roland JP-8080 Virtual Analog synthesizer. (1998)
  • Roland D-110 Linear Arithmetic synthesizer. (1988)
  • Ensoniq Mirage 8-bit sampler. (1984)
  • Akai MPC1000 sampler/sequencer. (2003)
